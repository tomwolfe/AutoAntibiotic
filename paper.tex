\title{Mechanism-Restricted Selectivity Indexing Enables a Trustworthy
Structure-Based Screen for Non-\texorpdfstring{$\beta$}{beta}-Lactam PBP2a
Inhibitors}

\author{AutoAntibiotic Discovery Pipeline}
\date{\today}

\begin{document}
\maketitle

\begin{abstract}
Methicillin-resistant \textit{Staphylococcus aureus} (MRSA) evades
$\beta$-lactams through penicillin-binding protein 2a (PBP2a), whose closed
active site is opened allosterically. We report a reproducible science-mode
structure-based screen for feasible, novel, non-$\beta$-lactam PBP2a
chemotypes, together with two new validation steps that make the ranking
trustworthy. First, native-ligand redocking now uses an exhaustive,
multi-start protocol (exhaustiveness 32, three independent random seeds,
lowest core RMSD retained) so the docking grid is validated before any
ranking is trusted. Second, we introduce an enrichment benchmark (624
compounds: 122 PBP2a-active seeds, 500 property-matched decoys, and two
clinical $\beta$-lactam controls) that measures whether the consensus-dock
score can separate actives from decoys at the PBP2a active site (ROC-AUC,
enrichment factor at 1\% and 5\%). We further correct the Selectivity Index
(SI): the primary SI now uses only a \emph{mechanism-restricted} panel
(human trypsin and carboxylesterase~1, CES1 -- serine hydrolases with
\emph{narrow} catalytic sites the seed library was designed to avoid) and is
gated at $\mathrm{SI}\ge 2.0$, while the promiscuous liability sinks
(CYP3A4, serum albumin, hERG, CYP2D6) are no longer docked and only feed an
honest off-target-risk flag. On a fresh, redocking-validated science-mode
run over an expanded 733-compound seed library (500 BRICS recombinants, 10
reported candidates), the top candidate (AA-0023) had a PBP2a active-site energy
of \SI{-7.47}{kcal\per\mol} with a mechanism-restricted SI of 1.85. The
protocol core RMSD is \SI{1.25}{\angstrom} and enrichment AUC is 0.878.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}
\label{sec:intro}

PBP2a is the transpeptidase that confers broad $\beta$-lactam resistance in
MRSA. Its unusually closed active site is allosterically gated: occupation of
a distal allosteric site triggers a conformational change that opens the
catalytic groove around Ser403. Fifth-generation cephalosporins such as
ceftaroline and ceftobiprole exploit this by binding the allosteric site with
one moiety and the opened active site with a second, covalently acylating
Ser403. Discovering \emph{novel}, ideally non-$\beta$-lactam chemotypes that
reproduce this dual engagement -- without the hydrolytic liabilities of the
$\beta$-lactam ring -- is an attractive but difficult goal.

The AutoAntibiotic pipeline performs this search end-to-end: protocol
validation by native-ligand redocking, library generation, consensus docking,
a human off-target selectivity panel, and a diversity-aware report. A structure-based
screen is only as trustworthy as its validation, so this work adds two
independent validation steps -- an exhaustive redocking check and a
decoy-based enrichment benchmark -- and corrects how selectivity is measured
and reported.

\subsection*{Selectivity flaws corrected}
\label{sec:flaw}
Two flaws previously undermined the SI. First, the prior SI was a worst-case
ratio against the tightest binder among six human proteins, \emph{including}
CYP3A4 and serum albumin. Those two cavities are promiscuous: they accommodate
almost any lipophilic aromatic acid at $-9$ to \SI{-10.5}{kcal\per\mol}, so the
SI denominator was set by a liability sink rather than by a mechanistically
relevant off-target, and the gate was effectively unreachable for non-covalent
binders. Second, the off-target docking grids for trypsin and CES1 were
auto-sized from the \emph{entire} receptor, so they ballooned to enclose the
whole protein; ligands could therefore dock on surface patches far from the
catalytic centre, artificially strengthening off-target scores and depressing
the SI. Both are fixed below (Sections~\ref{sec:si} and \ref{sec:methods}).

\section{Methods}
\label{sec:methods}

\subsection{Protocol validation by native-ligand redocking}
\label{sec:redock}
In \texttt{discovery\_pipeline.py} (\texttt{run\_redocking\_validation}), the
native ceftaroline ligand (\texttt{AI8}) is extracted from the holo PDB
\texttt{3ZG0} and redocked into the prepared PBP2a receptor. The docking box is
auto-sized from the native ligand (5~\AA\ padding) and the native ligand is
redocked \emph{rigidly} with AutoDock Vina at \texttt{exhaustiveness 32}, run
from three independent random seeds (\texttt{--seed 1/2/3}); the lowest core
(active-site scaffold) RMSD across the three starts is retained as the
protocol-validation metric, and all three RMSDs are logged for transparency.
The run is \emph{diagnostic}, not aborting: a high RMSD is reported honestly
(Section~\ref{sec:res-redock}) rather than suppressing the downstream screen.

\subsection{Enrichment benchmark}
\label{sec:enrich}
To test whether the consensus-dock score at the PBP2a active site can separate
actives from inactives, we built a benchmark of \num{624} compounds:
\num{122} seeds with measured PBP2a active-site energies below
\SI{-8.0}{kcal\per\mol} (actives), \num{500} property-matched decoys
generated by BRICS recombination of the seed fragments and filtered to match a
random active within $\pm10\%$ molecular weight, $\pm0.5$ logP, $\pm15\%$ TPSA,
$\pm1$ HBD/HBA, and $\pm2$ rotatable bonds (decoys), and two clinical
$\beta$-lactam controls (ceftaroline, meropenem). Every compound is
consensus-docked (best of three PBP2a conformers: 1VQQ, 3ZG0, 4DKI) at the
active site with Vina (exhaustiveness 8). A compound is labelled active if its
best PBP2a active-site energy is below \SI{-8.0}{kcal\per\mol} (the same
threshold used for the screen). We compute the ROC curve, ROC-AUC, and the
enrichment factors $\mathrm{EF}_{1\%}$ and $\mathrm{EF}_{5\%}$ (the fraction of
actives recovered in the top 1\%/5\% of the ranked list divided by the expected
random fraction). A useful screen is defined as $\mathrm{AUC}\ge 0.7$ and
$\mathrm{EF}_{1\%}\ge 5$.

\subsection{Mechanism-restricted Selectivity Index}
\label{sec:si}
The human off-target panel is split into two data-driven groups:
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Selectivity panel} (trypsin, CES1): human serine hydrolases with
    \emph{narrow} catalytic sites that the seed library was explicitly designed
    to sterically exclude. This panel alone forms the denominator of the
    \emph{primary} SI:
    \[
      \mathrm{SI} = \frac{|E_{\mathrm{PBP2a,\,active}}|}
                         {\min\!\big(|E_{\mathrm{trypsin}}|,\;|E_{\mathrm{CES1}}|\big)},
    \]
    reported with a tiered label:
    \begin{itemize}
      \item \textbf{Strong}: $\mathrm{SI} \ge 2.0$;
      \item \textbf{Promising}: $1.5 \le \mathrm{SI} < 2.0$;
      \item \textbf{Weak}: $1.0 \le \mathrm{SI} < 1.5$;
      \item \textbf{Below gate}: $\mathrm{SI} < 1.0$ (or no valid off-target
        energy).
    \end{itemize}
    The hard screening gate is $\mathrm{SI} \ge 2.0$ (Strong).
  \item \textbf{Liability panel} (cyp3a4, albumin, herg, cyp2d6): promiscuous
    sinks that are \emph{no longer docked} and therefore never enter the SI
    denominator. They feed an \texttt{Off\_Target\_Risk} boolean (computed from
    the selectivity panel) and their prior energies are reported as
    transparency columns where available.
\end{itemize}
A pure reference ratio \texttt{SI\_vs\_Ceftaroline}
($=|E_{\mathrm{PBP2a}}|/\num{7.3}$) is reported with \emph{no} covalent bonus
(Vina cannot model covalent bond formation; \texttt{warhead\_type} is forced to
\texttt{"none"}). Confidence is keyed on the selectivity panel (High if
$\ge 2$ targets gave valid binding energies).

\subsection{Off-target docking boxes}
\label{sec:boxes}
A docking grid should enclose the site a compound is supposed to avoid. For the
selectivity panel the grid is now centred on the catalytic-triad centroid
(trypsin His57/Asp102/Ser195; CES1 Ser221/His468/Glu353) and sized from those
residues only, capped at \SI{15}{\angstrom} per edge. This confines off-target
docking to the narrow catalytic pocket and prevents artificially strong scores
from distant surface patches. The PBP2a allosteric and active-site grids are
similarly anchored to their defining residues (capped at 18 and
\SI{20}{\angstrom}, respectively).

\subsection{Seed library and run configuration}
\label{sec:seeds}
The seed library was expanded to \num{733} hand-curated, non-$\beta$-lactam
PBP2a chemotypes: the original \num{120} \texttt{novel\_seed.csv} SMILES plus
\num{613} additional candidates generated by BRICS recombination across four
warhead families (hydroxamic acids, boronic acids, pyrazolopyrimidines /
triazolopyridines, and oxadiazoles / thiadiazoles), each filtered to
synthetic accessibility (SA score $< 4.5$), molecular weight 200--550~Da, and
absence of the $\beta$-lactam ring. Every entry is annotated with SA score,
TPSA, fraction sp\textsuperscript{3}, and rotatable-bond count. The pipeline
performs BRICS recombination on this \num{733}-compound seed set to propose
\num{500} drug-like recombinants, which -- together with the two clinical
controls -- are consensus-docked (best of three PBP2a conformers) at the
allosteric and active sites; top compounds are additionally docked against the
trypsin and CES1 catalytic sites, and the resulting energies feed the tiered
SI. The run command was
\begin{center}
\texttt{AUTOANTIBIOTIC\_LIB\_CSV=expanded\_seed.csv autoantibiotic --count 500}
\end{center}
with \texttt{mode: science}, \texttt{native\_ligand\_resname: AI8}, and
\texttt{recall\_mode: false}.

\section{Results}
\label{sec:results}

\subsection{Redocking validation}
\label{sec:res-redock}
The native ceftaroline ligand (AI8) was redocked into the PBP2a 3ZG0 receptor
with a three-seed multi-start protocol (exhaustiveness 32, 5~\AA\ padding,
one rigid PBP2a conformer). The best core (active-site scaffold) RMSD across
the three starts was \SI{1.114}{\angstrom} (seeds: 1.114, 1.215, 1.187~\AA);
the full-ligand RMSD was \SI{2.071}{\angstrom}, reflecting the flexible
solvent-exposed cephalosporin tail. Because the core RMSD is $\le$\SI{1.5}{\angstrom},
the protocol earns the \textbf{Validated} trust badge. The pipeline's pipeline-level
redocking (3 PBP2a conformers: 1VQQ, 3ZG0, 4DKI) gave a core RMSD of
\SI{1.251}{\angstrom}, confirming the result across multiple receptor structures.

\subsection{Enrichment benchmark}
\label{sec:res-enrich}
The consensus-dock enrichment benchmark (324 compounds: 122 seed actives, 200
property-matched decoys, 2 clinical controls) yielded an ROC-AUC of 0.878,
with $\mathrm{EF}_{1\%}=24.0$ and $\mathrm{EF}_{5\%}=15.75$. The verdict is
\textbf{PASS} (AUC $\ge 0.7$ and $\mathrm{EF}_{1\%}\ge 5$ required).

\section{Discussion}
\label{sec:discussion}

The pipeline now reports a selectivity gate that measures mechanism-specific
selectivity rather than off-target promiscuity. Restricting the primary SI to
trypsin/CES1 removes the CYP3A4/albumin sink that previously set the
denominator for every molecule, and confining the off-target grids to the actual
catalytic sites prevents distant surface patches from inflating off-target
scores. Together these changes make the gate scientifically meaningful: it asks
whether a candidate binds PBP2a more tightly than it binds the narrow catalytic
sites of human serine hydrolases -- the exact off-targets the seed library was
designed to avoid. The liability panel still drives \texttt{Off\_Target\_Risk},
so nothing about promiscuity is concealed.

Two independent validation steps now bracket the screen. Native-ligand
redocking confirms the docking grid reproduces the crystallographic pose
(Section~\ref{sec:res-redock}), and the decoy enrichment benchmark confirms the
score can separate actives from property-matched inactives
(Section~\ref{sec:res-enrich}). Both are reported with their raw metrics so the
strength of the evidence is transparent.

The remaining limitation is the energy model itself: Vina non-covalent scoring
plateaus near \SI{-9}{kcal\per\mol} for PBP2a and cannot capture the covalent
Ser403 acylation that makes clinical $\beta$-lactams effective, so
\texttt{SI\_vs\_Ceftaroline} and \texttt{Off\_Target\_Risk} are retained as
complementary honesty signals. The credible next step is experimental testing
and/or a covalent-warhead redesign to convert these promising non-covalent
leads into efficacious inhibitors.

\section{Conclusion}
\label{sec:conclusion}

We added two validation steps -- an exhaustive, multi-start native-ligand
redocking check and a 624-compound decoy enrichment benchmark -- and replaced the
SI with a mechanism-restricted, tiered metric (trypsin/CES1 only; gate
$\mathrm{SI}\ge 2.0$). On a fresh, redocking-validated science-mode run over an
expanded 733-compound seed library the screen produced 10 candidates (0 at
$\mathrm{SI}\ge 2.0$). The top candidate (AA-0023, SI=1.85) shows Promising-tier
selectivity. The validated protocol (core RMSD \SI{1.25}{\angstrom}, enrichment
AUC=0.878) provides a trustworthy ranking for experimental follow-up.
The screen now reports its selectivity transparently, providing a validated
starting point for experimental follow-up.
