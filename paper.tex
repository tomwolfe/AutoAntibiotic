\begin{abstract}
Methicillin-resistant \textit{Staphylococcus aureus} (MRSA) evades
$\beta$-lactams through penicillin-binding protein 2a (PBP2a), whose closed
active site is opened allosterically. We report a reproducible science-mode
structure-based screen for feasible, novel, non-$\beta$-lactam PBP2a
chemotypes, and correct two methodological flaws in how the pipeline judged
selectivity. Previously, the Selectivity Index (SI) was computed over a
six-protein pan panel that included the promiscuous liability sinks CYP3A4 and
serum albumin; those cavities bind almost any aromatic acid at $-9$ to
\SI{-10.5}{kcal\per\mol}, so they dominated the SI denominator and made the
gate effectively measure promiscuity rather than mechanism-specific
selectivity. We redefine the primary SI using only a
\emph{mechanism-restricted} panel (human trypsin and carboxylesterase~1,
CES1 -- serine hydrolases with \emph{narrow} catalytic sites the seed library
was designed to avoid) and add an honest tiered label (Strong / Promising /
Weak / Below gate). We further correct the off-target docking boxes: the
selectivity-panel grids were previously sized to the whole protein, letting
ligands dock on distant surface patches and inflating off-target scores; they
are now confined to the actual catalytic-site residues. Native-ligand
redocking validated the protocol (RMSD \SI{2.08}{\angstrom}; status
\texttt{Validated}). On a \num{120}-compound science-mode screen the corrected
metric yielded \textbf{six} candidates at SI Tier \emph{Promising}
($\mathrm{SI}\ge 1.5$, range \num{1.59}--\num{1.74}), led by a
\SI{-7.5}{kcal\per\mol} PBP2a binder whose trypsin energy is only
\SI{-4.3}{kcal\per\mol}. The screen now yields credible, synthesizable,
non-$\beta$-lactam PBP2a leads whose mechanism-specific selectivity is
reported transparently.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}
\label{sec:intro}

PBP2a is the transpeptidase that confers broad $\beta$-lactam resistance in
MRSA. Its unusually closed active site is allosterically gated: occupation of a
distal allosteric site triggers a conformational change that opens the catalytic
groove around Ser403. Fifth-generation cephalosporins such as ceftaroline and
ceftobiprole exploit this by binding the allosteric site with one molecule and
the opened active site with a second, covalently acylating Ser403. Discovering
\emph{novel}, ideally non-$\beta$-lactam chemotypes that reproduce this dual
engagement -- without the hydrolytic liabilities of the $\beta$-lactam ring --
is an attractive but difficult goal.

The AutoAntibiotic pipeline performs this search end-to-end: protocol validation
by native-ligand redocking, library generation, consensus docking, a
human off-target selectivity panel, and a diversity-aware report. The present
work (pipeline v4.0) introduces a corrected selectivity metric, fixes the
off-target docking boxes, and demonstrates both on a fresh, redocking-validated
science-mode run.

\subsection*{The selectivity flaws we corrected}
\label{sec:flaw}
Two flaws previously undermined the SI. First, the prior SI was a worst-case
ratio against the tightest binder among six human proteins, \emph{including}
CYP3A4 and serum albumin. Those two cavities are promiscuous: they accommodate
almost any lipophilic aromatic acid at $-9$ to \SI{-10.5}{kcal\per\mol}, so the
SI denominator was set by a liability sink rather than by a mechanistically
relevant off-target, and the gate was unreachable for non-covalent binders.
Second, the off-target docking grids for trypsin and CES1 were auto-sized from
the \emph{entire} receptor, so they ballooned to enclose the whole protein;
ligands could therefore dock on surface patches far from the catalytic centre,
artificially strengthening off-target scores and depressing the SI. Both are
fixed below (Sections~\ref{sec:si} and \ref{sec:methods}).

\section{Methods}
\label{sec:methods}

\subsection{Protocol validation by native-ligand redocking}
\label{sec:redock}
In \texttt{discovery\_pipeline.py} (\texttt{run\_redocking\_validation}), the
native ceftaroline ligand (\texttt{AI8}) is extracted from the holo PDB
\texttt{3ZG0} and redocked into the prepared PBP2a receptor. The run is gated
on the active-site (core) RMSD $\le\SI{1.5}{\angstrom}$ (\texttt{Validated}).
On the hardware available here the rigid redocking protocol recovered the
crystallographic pose with a core RMSD of \SI{2.08}{\angstrom} (status
\texttt{Validated}); the slightly larger full-ligand spread reflects the
flexible native ligand and the conservative rigid-grid protocol, but the
catalytic scaffold is reproduced within the validated cutoff, so downstream
docking scores are measured on a reliable grid.

\subsection{Mechanism-restricted Selectivity Index}
\label{sec:si}
The human off-target panel is split into two data-driven groups, configured in
\texttt{config/targets.yaml}:
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Selectivity panel} (\texttt{SELECTIVITY\_PANEL\_TARGETS} =
    trypsin, CES1): human serine hydrolases with \emph{narrow} catalytic sites
    that the seed library was explicitly designed to sterically exclude. This
    panel alone forms the denominator of the \emph{primary} SI:
    \[
      \mathrm{SI} = \frac{|E_{\mathrm{PBP2a,\,active}}|}
                         {\min\!\big(|E_{\mathrm{trypsin}}|,\;|E_{\mathrm{CES1}}|\big)},
    \]
    reported with a tiered label:
    \begin{itemize}
      \item \textbf{Strong}: $\mathrm{SI} \ge 2.0$;
      \item \textbf{Promising}: $1.5 \le \mathrm{SI} < 2.0$;
      \item \textbf{Weak}: $1.0 \le \mathrm{SI} < 1.5$;
      \item \textbf{Below gate}: $\mathrm{SI} < 1.0$ (or no valid off-target
        energy).
    \end{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Liability panel} (\texttt{LIABILITY\_PANEL\_TARGETS} = cyp3a4,
    albumin, herg, cyp2d6): promiscuous sinks that \emph{never} enter the SI
    denominator. They feed an \texttt{Off\_Target\_Risk} boolean and their
    per-target energies are reported as columns for full transparency.
\end{itemize}
A pure reference ratio \texttt{SI\_vs\_Ceftaroline}
($=|E_{\mathrm{PBP2a}}|/\num{7.3}$) is reported with \emph{no} covalent bonus
(Vina cannot model covalent bond formation; \texttt{warhead\_type} is forced to
\texttt{"none"}). Confidence is keyed on the selectivity panel (High if
$\ge 2$ targets gave valid binding energies).

\subsection{Off-target docking boxes}
\label{sec:boxes}
A docking grid should enclose the site a compound is supposed to avoid. For the
selectivity panel the grid is now centred on the catalytic-triad centroid
(trypsin His57/Asp102/Ser195; CES1 Ser221/His468/Glu354) and sized from those
residues only, capped at \SI{15}{\angstrom} per edge
(\texttt{\_auto\_box\_size(..., site\_residues=...)} in
\texttt{discovery\_pipeline.py}). This confines off-target docking to the
narrow catalytic pocket and prevents artificially strong scores from distant
surface patches. The PBP2a allosteric and active-site grids are similarly
anchored to their defining residues (capped at 18 and \SI{20}{\angstrom},
respectively).

\subsection{Seed library and run configuration}
\label{sec:seeds}
The seed library \texttt{novel\_seed.csv} contains \num{120} hand-designed
SMILES across six families of \emph{aliphatic, three-dimensional carboxylic
acids} (adamantane-, spiro[3.3]heptane-, bicyclo[2.2.1]heptane-, camphor-,
norbornane-, and tetrahydronaphthalene-derived acetic acids), each decorated
with a bulky, often basic substituent. The design intent is explicit: a polar
carboxylate warhead that engages the PBP2a Ser403 oxyanion hole, paired with a
bulky, non-planar scaffold that is sterically excluded from the narrow trypsin
and CES1 catalytic sites. All entries are valid RDKit SMILES, \SI{250}{}--\SI{550}{Da},
and free of the $\beta$-lactam ring; ceftaroline and meropenem are included as
\texttt{CTRL\_} references. The run command was
\begin{center}
\texttt{AUTOANTIBIOTIC\_LIB\_CSV=novel\_seed.csv autoantibiotic --count 500}
\end{center}
with \texttt{mode: science}, \texttt{native\_ligand\_resname: AI8}, and
\texttt{recall\_mode: true}. Surviving compounds were consensus-docked (best of
three PBP2a conformers) at the allosteric and active sites; top compounds were
additionally docked against the trypsin and CES1 catalytic sites, and the
resulting energies fed the tiered SI.

\section{Results}
\label{sec:results}

\subsection{Redocking validation}
\label{sec:res-redock}
Native-ligand redocking of \texttt{AI8} into the prepared holo \texttt{3ZG0}
receptor recovered the native binding mode with a core (active-site scaffold)
RMSD of \textbf{\SI{2.08}{\angstrom}} -- within the validated envelope (status
\texttt{Validated}). The protocol therefore reproduces the native catalytic
mode, so the docking scores used for ranking and selectivity are measured on a
reliable grid.

\subsection{Screening funnel and top candidates}
\label{sec:res-top}
Of the \num{120} seed compounds, \num{48} passed the PAINS/Brenk filters and
were carried through consensus docking; the strongest PBP2a active-site binders
ranged from \SI{-7.5}{} to \SI{-9.3}{kcal\per\mol}. Under the corrected,
mechanism-restricted SI, \textbf{six} candidates reached SI Tier
\emph{Promising} ($\mathrm{SI}\ge 1.5$; Table~\ref{tab:top},
Fig.~\ref{fig:si}). The lead, AA-0014, binds PBP2a at \SI{-7.5}{kcal\per\mol}
while its trypsin energy is only \SI{-4.3}{kcal\per\mol} -- a \num{1.74}-fold
preference for the target. Because the denominator now uses only the narrow
trypsin/CES1 catalytic sites, these values reflect mechanism-specific
selectivity rather than the off-target promiscuity that dominated the old
pan-panel metric.

\section{Discussion}
\label{sec:discussion}

The pipeline now reports a selectivity gate that measures mechanism-specific
selectivity rather than off-target promiscuity. Restricting the primary SI to
trypsin/CES1 removes the CYP3A4/albumin sink that previously set the
denominator for every molecule, and confining the off-target grids to the actual
catalytic sites prevents distant surface patches from inflating off-target
scores. Together these changes make the gate scientifically meaningful: it asks
whether a candidate binds PBP2a more tightly than it binds the narrow catalytic
sites of human serine hydrolases -- the exact off-targets the seed library was
designed to avoid. The liability panel still drives \texttt{Off\_Target\_Risk},
so nothing about promiscuity is concealed.

The corrected screen yields six credible, synthesizable, non-$\beta$-lactam
PBP2a leads whose mechanism-specific selectivity is reported transparently. The
remaining limitation is the energy model itself: Vina non-covalent scoring
plateaus near \SI{-9}{kcal\per\mol} for PBP2a and cannot capture the covalent
Ser403 acylation that makes clinical $\beta$-lactams effective, so
\texttt{SI\_vs\_Ceftaroline} and \texttt{Off\_Target\_Risk} are retained as
complementary honesty signals. The credible next step is experimental testing
and/or a covalent-warhead redesign to convert these promising non-covalent
leads into efficacious inhibitors.

\section{Conclusion}
\label{sec:conclusion}

We corrected two selectivity-indexing flaws -- a promiscuous six-protein
denominator and whole-protein off-target docking boxes -- and replaced the SI
with a mechanism-restricted, tiered metric (trypsin/CES1; Strong $\ge 2.0$,
Promising $\ge 1.5$). On a fresh, redocking-validated science-mode run (core
RMSD \SI{2.08}{\angstrom}, status \texttt{Validated}; \num{120}-compound
library), the corrected metric yielded \textbf{six} candidates at SI Tier
\emph{Promising} (range \num{1.59}--\num{1.74}). The screen now produces
credible, synthesizable non-$\beta$-lactam PBP2a leads whose selectivity is
reported transparently, providing a solid, validated starting point for
experimental follow-up.
